Scene 1: Andrew’s Proposal

[Interior – Tavern, late night. Most guests have left. Rain taps softly against the shutters. Andrew stands behind the counter, polishing a cup he has already cleaned twice.]

MC approaches.

MC
You said there was a way to stop them.

Andrew
There is. Or rather… there may be.
After Page fell, I searched the old records again. Cross-checked them with what I remembered, and with what I had tried very hard to forget.

MC
You found something.

Andrew
Not something. A door.
And a key.

[Andrew places a small wrapped bundle on the table. He unfolds the cloth. Inside is a handful of dark silver-grey ash. It faintly glimmers.]

MC
Ash?

Andrew
Not ordinary ash. This was hidden in the cave where the fragments first thickened.
Burnt knowledge. The residue of a failed purification long before your time.
It is one of the few materials that can answer the First Monolith without being devoured by it.

MC
And this can stop the monsters?

Andrew
If forged correctly, yes. It can open the sealed path to the source beneath the Monolith.
That is where the dark side took root. That is where the fragments are born.
Destroy that corruption, and the tide may finally break.

MC
“May.”

Andrew
I deal in truth where I can. Certainty belongs to priests and fools.

[Andrew wraps the ash again, carefully.]

Andrew
You have endured more than most. More importantly, you have not broken.
That matters more than strength. The source tears open the weak in mind before it kills the body.

MC
And you think I can survive it.

Andrew
I think you may be the first one in years who can reach it at all.

4. Post-Boss Revelation Cutscene
   Scene: After the Inner Shadow Falls

[The core chamber grows quiet. Dust and black ash drift through the air. The source now hangs suspended in clear white-gold light. Andrew steps toward it slowly, almost reverently.]

MC
It’s over.

Andrew
No.
Not over.

[Andrew lifts a shaking hand toward the light. The source responds. Rings of script begin to revolve around him.]

MC
Andrew?

Andrew
Do you hear it?

MC
Hear what?

Andrew
Silence.
For the first time in years, I can hear silence beneath the screaming.

[Andrew laughs once — not with joy, but with exhausted disbelief.]

Andrew
I had almost forgotten what it sounded like.

MC
What are you doing?

Andrew
What I should have done long ago.

[He turns. His face is calm, but brighter than before, almost feverish.]

Andrew Reveal Dialogue

Andrew
You deserve the truth, at least.
I was not merely a scholar. Not merely a founder.
I was the architect of the First Monolith.

MC
You built it.

Andrew
I conceived it. Raised it. Taught it how to gather, how to order, how to answer.
It was meant to be a spine for civilisation — not a shrine, not a prison, not a throne for frightened kings.

MC
Then all of this—

Andrew
Came after.
Ambition. Worship. Doctrine. Purity. Fear.
The Precursors fed it their vanity. The rulers fed it their hunger.
They took a structure and stuffed it with poison, then called the poison destiny.

MC
And now what?
You repair it and hand it back to another king?

Andrew
No.
This time there will be no theft. No puppet ruler draped in ceremony. No council of parasites.
I will restore the source myself. I will bind the Precursors beneath it. I will end this war, break the fragments, and cut the rot from the world at its root.

MC
By ruling it.

Andrew
By ordering it. Properly.

MC
That’s the same thing.

Andrew
Is it?
Look outside this chamber.
Civil war. Bandit lords calling themselves liberators. Officials too weak to govern. Priests feeding lies to the hungry.
You have seen what freedom becomes in a starving land.
Every chain I hated has returned wearing a different name.

MC
So you want to become the next king.

Andrew
Call it what you like.
I will treat Aldrens and Precursors as equals under law. I will not restore the old privilege.
But there must be one will above the chaos. One centre that cannot be bribed, frightened, or forgotten.

MC
A saviour.

Andrew
No.
A necessity.

#MC Response

With someone saying they alone can carry the burden.

Then the world forgets how to stand without them.

Idealism. Here? After all you’ve seen?

Andrew

MC
Even then.

Andrew—

No king descended from the stone.
No saviour rose from the dark.

The hunger would not vanish.
The hatred would not vanish.

Maybe they would build something better.
Maybe they would fail.
Maybe they would beg for chains again.

But if this land was ever to belong to its people, then it could not be carried forever by a king, a machine, or a man who claimed to know better.

There is no saviour in this world.

If people are to be saved, they must save themselves.
