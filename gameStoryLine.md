# Game Storyline

## Overview

The story opens in a style inspired by *One Hundred Years of Solitude*: many years later, when Bruce faces the choice that will decide the fate of the Aldren, he remembers the distant afternoon when people first discovered that Heaven had gone silent.

People rush to churches, pray, and ask the preachers for help, hoping God has not abandoned them. No answer comes. Fear and hopelessness spread through the crowds.

In the following days, monsters appear in the cities. They absorb people's knowledge, leaving their victims as mindless creatures. The survivors flee to the capital for shelter. This day becomes known as **the Great Silence**.

## Setting

- **Capital:** Aureth
- **Bruce's hometown:** Dawnmere
- **Main character:** Bruce
- **Main conflict:** The First Monolith, the knowledge fragments, and the hidden struggle between the Aldren and the Precursors.

## Core Premise

Three years after the Great Silence, Bruce settles in a new city and becomes a hunter. His job is to fight monsters in the outskirts and deliver their bodies to the government for payment.

The government recycles the monsters' absorbed knowledge, now called **knowledge fragments**, and returns those fragments to the **First Monolith** to slow its decay.

Now, the gears begin to turn.

## Main Locations

### Forest

In the forest, the player can:

- Talk to other hunters.
- Talk to seekers, who are government-employed hunters.
- Talk to Officer Page, who is responsible for recycling monsters.
- Explore different areas.

### Tavern

In the tavern, the player can:

- Talk to Andrew, the barkeeper, to gather information.
- Buy properties.
- Join competitions.
- Talk with other NPCs to discover more about the city.

## Main Characters

- **Bruce:** The main character and a hunter of knowledge fragments.
- **Andrew:** The barkeeper. He knows many of the city's secrets and later reveals that he is a Precursor.
- **Page:** A government officer responsible for recycling monsters. He later betrays the government and becomes a boss.
- **Charles:** The new officer who replaces Page after Page becomes wanted.
- **The Precursors:** The rulers who built the First Monolith.
- **The Aldren:** The native people of the land.
- **The Aldren Liberators:** A rebel group fighting against the Precursors.

## Plot Progression

### Opening

Many years later, when the fate of the Aldren would again be placed in his hands, Bruce would remember that distant afternoon when people first discovered that Heaven had gone silent.

Bells rang across the land. Mothers carried children into chapels. Old men fell to their knees in the streets. The preachers lifted their voices until they broke, begging for one sign that God had not abandoned His people.

No answer came.

And when fear had exhausted prayer, the first monsters emerged...

### First Scene

Three years later, Bruce wakes in a narrow bed above the tavern. Although years have passed, the catastrophe still lingers like a wound from yesterday. Time flies, memories dim, and the old world slips further away.

Now Bruce begins his new life as a hunter of knowledge fragments.

### Level 5: Memory and Betrayal

After reaching level 5, Bruce recalls some of his lost memories. Most people lost many of their memories after the Great Silence.

At the same time, Page is revealed to have betrayed the government. He secretly kept monsters, absorbed their knowledge, and attacked another officer with a group of monsters. He becomes wanted by the government, turns into a level 15 boss, and escapes to the outskirts.

Hunters are called to find and arrest him. Charles appears in the forest and replaces Page as the new officer.

Bruce goes to the tavern to speak with Andrew, who is knowledgeable and always seems to know the city's secrets. Andrew says something similar happened in the past, so he knows how to find Page. He believes Page has become the leader of a group of knowledge fragments.

Andrew warns that facing such a monster is dangerous. The player must reach at least level 15 before confronting Page.

### Level 15: Confronting Page

After reaching level 15 and speaking with Andrew, Bruce can find Page with Andrew's help.

After Page is defeated, he tells Bruce the truth: the First Monolith was not built to guide or manage the Aldren. It was built by the Precursors to control them.

Page says he discovered this truth and chose to revolt against the Precursors. He joined the Aldren Liberators and stole knowledge fragments to help liberate the Aldren.

Page then asks whether Bruce wants to join them and revolt against the Precursors.

- If Bruce says yes, Page launches a surprise attack.
- If Bruce says no, Page pretends to show Bruce something, then launches a surprise attack.

Andrew saves Bruce and defeats Page. Other monsters arrive and absorb Page, becoming stronger. Andrew and Bruce are forced to withdraw immediately.

### After Page's Defeat

Bruce and Andrew return to the forest and tell Charles what happened. They warn him that stronger monsters are appearing in the forest.

Charles says the government cannot offer more help because most of its power is being used to suppress the Aldren rebellion.

Back in the tavern, Andrew tells Bruce a story about someone who once entered the First Monolith, defeated the dark side of knowledge, and saved the world. He says that to complete this task, the warrior must be wise enough and ready to sacrifice.

Andrew also tells Bruce that he does not have to fight for the Precursors. Bruce can choose between a peaceful life and challenging the dark side of knowledge.

Before the final challenge, the player can read Andrew's books to learn more about:

- The history of the Aldren.
- The history of the Precursors.
- The First Monolith.
- The disaster and its origins.

If the player is above level 20, Bruce can speak to Andrew and begin the final challenge.

### Level 20: The Final Challenge

Bruce and Andrew enter the First Monolith and encounter the dark side of knowledge, a level 25 enemy.

After they defeat it, the inside of the Monolith fills with light. Andrew is glad and says he has waited a long time for this moment.

Andrew then reveals the truth: he is a Precursor, the builder of the First Monolith, and was banished by the nobles of the Precursors.

Now, he wants revenge.

Andrew says the nobles have become corrupt and ignored the suffering of the Aldren. He wants to modify the Monolith and use it to control the nobles. He says he and Bruce can become the new lords and build a new nation where the Aldren and the Precursors share equal rights.

## Endings

### Ending 1: Bruce Agrees With Andrew

If Bruce agrees with Andrew, they modify the Monolith and become the new lords.

The monsters are gradually eliminated by seekers and hunters. No new monsters are created because the dark side of knowledge, their source, has been defeated.

However, the people are still controlled by the First Monolith. No one knows how many years this control will last.

### Ending 2: Bruce Refuses and Defeats Andrew

If Bruce refuses Andrew, Andrew becomes the final boss at level 20.

If Bruce defeats Andrew, Bruce destroys the core of the First Monolith and releases its knowledge to all people. Everyone learns how the disaster happened and how they were governed.

Andrew argues that the Aldren cannot manage themselves and must rely on the guidance of the First Monolith.

Bruce believes people should be independent, and destroying the core is the first step. There will be chaos at first, but it is time for the Aldren to grow and govern themselves.

God is not smart. Humans are.

The future is unseen.

### Ending 3: Bruce Refuses and Loses to Andrew

If Bruce is defeated by Andrew, Bruce dies and is buried in the First Monolith.

Andrew becomes the new lord and honors Bruce as the hero of the Aldren.
